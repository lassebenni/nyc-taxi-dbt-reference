# Exercise: Lakehouse synthesis write-up

**Chapter:** Week 13, [The lakehouse idea](https://github.com/lassebenni/datatrack/blob/main/Data%20Track/Week%2013/week_13__1_intro_lakehouse.md) (synthesis across the week)

## Goal

Write a short note (about half a page) you could send to a teammate explaining what you did this week: what the lakehouse is, why you moved the dbt project to Databricks, and one thing that surprised you.

This exercise is writing only — no code to run. Use your own experience from the four content chapters and the earlier practice exercises.

## Your task

Fill in `WRITEUP.md`:

1. **Lakehouse in one paragraph** — storage + table format + compute, in plain language.
2. **Why Databricks for dbt** — one paragraph connecting the Week 10 project to this week's port (scale, Delta, shared catalog).
3. **One surprise** — a concrete moment from class or your own runs (timing, data quality, Unity Catalog, PySpark laziness, etc.).

Aim for 250-400 words total. Portfolio-ready tone: clear, specific, no buzzword salad.

## Success criteria

- A teammate who missed the week could read your note and understand what you built and why.
- You mention both PySpark/notebook work **and** the dbt port — this week had two transformation tools.
- The surprise is specific (a number, a test result, a UI moment), not generic ("Spark is fast").
